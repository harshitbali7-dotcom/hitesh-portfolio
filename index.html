Flagship case study · 2026

Olinia — go-to-market for a Mexico-focused urban EV initiative

Olinia means "movement" in Nahuatl. This sanitized academic strategy case explores an ultra-affordable L7 micro-EV concept designed for dense-city mobility in Mexico.

I built the strategic master plan: who to sell to, how to reach them, and how to make the economics work for underbanked urban drivers. The answer was a financing-first, TCO-led approach — anchored on trusted retail access, executed through lean Experience Hubs, and scaled with digital direct. Public portfolio version is sanitized and excludes confidential client materials.

Margin model
scenario-based unit economics and channel margin analysis
3
channel GTM: retail · hubs · digital
Financing-first
credit-access strategy for underbanked and fleet buyers
TCO-led
price, LTV, and 36-month payment scenario modeling

01Insight

Short urban trips, brutal fuel costs, and a large solvent-but-underbanked driver base. The job to be done wasn't "buy a car" — it was "earn more per day and own faster."

02Strategy

Anchor on SUPERISSSTE for government-backed trust and credit access; lead with TCO and same-day financing; sequence the rollout cargo → fleets → retail → ride-hailing to de-risk launch.

03Execution

Replace high-CAPEX dealerships with hub-and-spoke Experience Hubs for delivery and service, plus a financing partner ecosystem (SOFOMs, Coppel, Kavak) matched to each customer profile.
